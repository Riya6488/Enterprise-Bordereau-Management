# BDX Analytics Platform (Microsoft Fabric)

## 🧩 Objective
Move beyond tracking to advanced analytics and business intelligence on bordereaux data.

## 🏗️ Data Engineering (Fabric)
✅ Multi-layer architecture:

🔹 Bronze Layer - Raw BDX files ingestion

🔹 Silver Layer - Data cleaning and transformation; Schema standardization

🔹 Gold Layer - Analytical data model; Business-ready datasets

_👉 Implements modern lakehouse architecture_

## 🧠 Analytical Model

Built with:

- Proper relationships
- Cross-filter directions
- Optimized star schema for reporting




## 📊 Advanced Analytics & KPIs
Key Metrics Developed:

- New policy count
- Active policy count
- Total written premium
- Claims analysis
- Geography-based insights
- Line of business segmentation
- BDX type analysis


### 🧮 Advanced DAX

- Heavy rule-based calculations
- Derived metrics and KPIs
- Time-based analysis

_👉 Demonstrates strong analytics modeling capability_

## 📈 Visualization Layer

- KPI Cards
- Trend Analysis Charts
- Distribution graphs
- Performance dashboards

_👉 Enables decision-making insights for underwriting and risk teams_

## 📈 Business Impact
### ✅ Operational Improvements

- Centralized tracking of BDX submissions
- Reduced manual monitoring effort
- Improved visibility into missing data


### ✅ Compliance & Risk Management

- Early identification of delayed or missing BDX submissions
- Supports contractual compliance tracking


### ✅ Strategic Insights 

- Enables data-driven underwriting decisions
- Provides visibility into portfolio performance
- Identifies potential contractual breaches


### ✅ Scalability & Modernization

- Transition from reporting → analytics platform
- Adoption of Microsoft Fabric for scalable data solutions


## 🛠️ Tech Stack

- DAX – Business logic & calculations
- Microsoft Fabric – Data lakehouse & analytics
- CI/CD (Power BI Service) – Deployment automation

All identifiers anonymized
Logic preserved for demonstration purposes_
